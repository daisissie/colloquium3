About Memory Landscapes

The project explores how to turn non-spatial data—memories and written stories—into spatial maps. I am investigating how diaries, memoirs can reveal hidden insights about people and places when shown on a map. Memories are personal and often very fuzzy and blurry, but they hold details that shape our identities and our sense of place.

This idea is sort of inspired by the art game “exquisite corpse”. When clicking on each spot, the text shown may be describing a life/situation completely different. Aiming to show how personal memories can shape our understanding of places, connecting individual experiences with the physical world.
